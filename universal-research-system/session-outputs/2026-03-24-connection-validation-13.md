## Discarded Connections

### Melatonin → Calprotectin and Lactoferrin
**Reason:** Melatonin-marked EC cell/dysbiosis state does not directly drive neutrophilic calprotectin/lactoferrin release; melatonin immune-modulating functions are primarily anti-inflammatory, opposing calprotectin elevation; FDN clinical data demonstrates dissociation (normal calprotectin with dysbiosis markers). Mirrors the established pattern of non-neutrophilic variables discarded from calprotectin connections.

### Melatonin → Anti-gliadin IgA
**Reason:** No direct structural connection between EC cell-derived noon melatonin and anti-gliadin IgA; the proposed causal pathway (dysbiosis to permeability to gliadin translocation to AGA-IgA) requires multiple intervening variables without independent structural evidence at Relevant confidence; AGA-IgA is primarily determined by gliadin exposure and sIgA status, neither directly determined by noon melatonin. Mirrors the Systemic Oxidative Stress to Anti-gliadin IgA discard pattern.

### Melatonin → Occult Blood
**Reason:** No structural connection between EC cell-derived melatonin elevation (a functional gut ecology marker) and structural mucosal bleeding required for occult blood detection; melatonin has no documented mechanism for producing structural GI lesions. Mirrors the established Occult Blood discard pattern.

### Dysbiosis-Intestinal Permeability Feedback Loop → Occult Blood
**Reason:** DIPFL-mediated hyperpermeability is a functional paracellular opening mechanism mechanistically distinct from structural mucosal bleeding; both FDN case patients show zero/normal occult blood despite extensive DIPFL-indicating dysbiotic and permeability markers; structural mucosal lesions with vascular involvement are required for occult blood detection, not functional permeability changes.

### Mucosal Immune Tolerance and sIgA Function → Calprotectin and Lactoferrin
**Reason:** Clinical FDN data demonstrates dissociation: both case patients show depleted sIgA (359, 312) with normal calprotectin (91, 6); the mucosal immune dysfunction pathway from sIgA depletion primarily engages eosinophil EDN/EPX compensation rather than neutrophilic calprotectin response as documented in FDN source material; insufficient independent evidence at Relevant confidence for direct sIgA-calprotectin structural connection.

### Mucosal Immune Tolerance and sIgA Function → Occult Blood
**Reason:** sIgA depletion enables pathogen colonization and mucosal immune dysfunction but does not specifically produce structural mucosal bleeding; both FDN case patients show depleted sIgA with zero/normal occult blood; structural pathology (polyps, ulcers, cancer) causing occult blood is mechanistically distinct from sIgA-mediated mucosal immune dysfunction. Mirrors established Occult Blood discard pattern.

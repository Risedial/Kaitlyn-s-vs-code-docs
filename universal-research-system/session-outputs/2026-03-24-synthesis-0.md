## Validated Variables (33 total)

### Indican
**Confidence band:** core  |  **Finding count:** 5
Urinary indican (indoxyl sulfate) is produced when anaerobic bacteria act on undigested tryptophan in the small intestine, generating indole that is absorbed via the hepatic portal system, conjugated by the liver, and excreted in urine. Elevated indican reliably signals dysbiosis and/or protein maldigestion, with downstream consequences including amino acid depletion, neurotransmitter imbalance, glycemic dysregulation, and hepatic congestion. High-to-very-high results additionally implicate hypochlorhydria, protease enzyme deficiency, and hypomotility. The marker serves as a pointer toward SIBO, leaky gut, and liver burden simultaneously.

### Urinary Bile Acids
**Confidence band:** core  |  **Finding count:** 5
Urinary bile acids (UBAs/TBAs) represent bile acids not cleared by the liver on first pass through the hepatic portal system, spilling into systemic circulation and filtered by the kidneys. Elevated UBAs signal liver parenchymal damage or congestion from excessive antigen/toxin/immune complex load, or poor enterohepatic circulation. Very low UBAs indicate opposite problem: poor bile flow, gallbladder dysfunction, or IBD with impaired reabsorption. The marker sits at the intersection of digestion, detoxification, and immune function because bile acids serve cholesterol elimination, fat absorption, glucose regulation, and antimicrobial functions simultaneously.

### 8-OHdG
**Confidence band:** core  |  **Finding count:** 5
Urinary 8-OHdG is an oxidized derivative of deoxyguanosine formed when reactive oxygen species attack guanine bases in nuclear and mitochondrial DNA. It is the most commonly observed single nucleotide-base lesion in oxidative DNA damage and functions as a cumulative biomarker of cellular oxidative stress. Elevated 8-OHdG reflects ongoing DNA damage exceeding antioxidant repair capacity, accelerating aging, promoting mutation, and increasing degenerative disease and cancer risk. In FDN it serves as the 'overarching marker' because oxidative stress amplifies every other dysfunction simultaneously.

### Zonulin
**Confidence band:** core  |  **Finding count:** 5
Zonulin is the primary physiological regulator of tight junctions (TJs) in the small intestinal epithelium, released in response to bacterial colonization, antigen exposure, and gliadin. Sustained Zonulin elevation produces chronic intestinal hyperpermeability, systemic antigen/toxin translocation, humoral immune activation, autoimmune disease, and blood-brain barrier compromise. It is bidirectionally connected to both the stress-HPA axis (cortisol activates mast cells that release histamine, which triggers Zonulin) and the gut microbiome (dysbiosis is the primary physiological trigger). Permeability changes measurable by Zonulin can precede clinical symptoms by up to one year.

### Histamine â€” MBA
**Confidence band:** core  |  **Finding count:** 5
Histamine is a biogenic amine with 23 documented physiological functions including local immune response, neurotransmission, gastric acid secretion, and vascular regulation. In the FDN MBA context, elevated histamine reflects either excess production/ingestion (SIBO, histamine-rich foods, mast cell activation from stress) or impaired degradation by DAO. Elevated histamine in the lamina propria directly causes vascular permeability changes and drives Zonulin release, creating bidirectional amplification with leaky gut. Clinical effects scale dose-dependently from GI stimulation through tachycardia, bronchospasm, to cardiac arrest. Cortisol directly activates gut mast cells to release histamine, establishing the HPA-histamine stress link.

### DAO (Diamine Oxidase)
**Confidence band:** core  |  **Finding count:** 5
Diamine oxidase (DAO) is the principal histamine-degrading enzyme in the intestinal mucosa, synthesized exclusively by mucosal cells in the crypts of intestinal villi. Blood DAO serves as both a histamine clearance marker and an intestinal mucosal integrity marker â€” elevated in early mucosal injury (compensatory upregulation) and low in chronic injury (crypt cell destruction). DAO activity requires cofactors vitamin B6, vitamin C, copper, and manganese; deficiency of any one reduces enzymatic function independent of DAO protein synthesis. Very low DAO (<8.9 ng/ml) is consistently observed in IBD, Crohn's, UC, MS, and rheumatoid arthritis, reflecting the crypt destruction characteristic of these conditions.

### Systemic Oxidative Stress Cascade
**Confidence band:** core  |  **Finding count:** 5
Systemic oxidative stress occurs when reactive oxygen species (ROS) and reactive nitrogen species (RNS) production overwhelms antioxidant defense systems. In FDN it is the 'overarching contributor' to metabolic chaos because it simultaneously amplifies every other systemic dysfunction â€” impairing enzyme activity, damaging cell membranes, destroying DNA, disrupting hormonal signaling, accelerating aging, and promoting carcinogenesis. It is both a consequence of metabolic dysfunction and an active worsener of those same dysfunctions, creating a self-perpetuating cascade. 8-OHdG measures its DNA-level expression and lipid peroxides (MDA) measure its cell membrane-level expression.

### Hepatic Detoxification Impairment
**Confidence band:** core  |  **Finding count:** 5
Hepatic detoxification impairment describes the state where the liver's Phase I (oxidation/reduction/hydrolysis), Phase II (conjugation), and Phase III (elimination) processing capacity is overwhelmed by incoming toxin, antigen, hormone, and immune complex load. The liver is described in FDN as 'Grand Central Station' performing ~500 functions including detoxification, bile production, blood sugar regulation, immune function, and thyroid hormone conversion. ### Histamine-DAO Regulatory System
**Confidence band:** core  |  **Finding count:** 5

### H. pylori
**Confidence band:** core  |  **Finding count:** 4
H. pylori is a spiral gram-negative bacterium that colonizes the gastric mucosa by burrowing into the stomach lining.

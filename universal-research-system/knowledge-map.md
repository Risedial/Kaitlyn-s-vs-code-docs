## Variable: Urinary Bile Acids
**Confidence band:** core
**Summary:** Urinary bile acids (UBAs/TBAs) represent bile acids not cleared by the liver on first pass through the hepatic portal system, spilling into systemic circulation and filtered by the kidneys. Elevated UBAs signal liver parenchymal damage or congestion from excessive antigen/toxin/immune complex load, or poor enterohepatic circulation. Very low UBAs indicate opposite problem: poor bile flow, gallbladder dysfunction, or IBD with impaired reabsorption. The marker sits at the intersection of digestion, detoxification, and immune function because bile acids serve cholesterol elimination, fat absorption, glucose regulation, and antimicrobial functions simultaneously.
**Reasoning chain:** Elevated UBAs directly indicate failed first-pass hepatic portal bile acid clearance, constituting a measurable marker of liver parenchymal congestion or damage. [Core] | Elevated UBAs produce metabolic consequences including unstable blood sugar, dyslipidemia, and inability to lose weight because bile acids regulate lipid and glucose metabolism via FXR and TGR5 receptors. [Core] | Very low UBAs indicate poor bile flow, missing/congested gallbladder, or disrupted enterohepatic circulation, associated with IBD, diarrhea, and malabsorption. [Core]

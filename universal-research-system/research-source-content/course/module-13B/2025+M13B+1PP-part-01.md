## Page 5

Module 13B Lesson 1 2025-11-19
The 90-Day Protocol Framework
The 90-Day Supplement Protocol is not about
specific products.
Instead, it’s about organizational categories that
help you understand and use supplementation.
It has a starter set of supplements to give you
examples for each category, and a way to
practice writing protocols, but our intention is
never to limit the type or brand of supplements
you use in practice once you graduate.
Think of this 90-Day Framework as a map for
clinical thinking, not a rigid recipe for client
protocols.

Category 6: Vital Voids
Purpose:
To support functions the body
can no longer perform on its
own.
These are “permanent gaps”
in physiology, often due to

## Page 12

Module 13B Lesson 1 2025-11-19
Putting It All Together
Lab results , lifestyle, and clinical correlation drives decisions → every recommendation
connects back to the data and the person.
Apply findings across all categories of supplementation to see which are categories are
relevant and which supplements are needed.
Introduce products in phases of care:
•Relief → quick wins, symptom support, vital voids
•Corrective → restore function, address underlying issues
•Maintenance → sustain gains, long-term balance
Sequence protocols strategically → drip products in gradually, adjust as needed, and pace
according to client tolerance and compliance

Diet
Remove food sensitivities to lower stress response
Avoid soy because of phytoestrogens

Ensure adequate protein and healthy fat in diet to regulate sugar handling
Source animal based foods grass-fed or pastured and organic as much as possible
Avoid drinking and eating food from plastic containers (estrogenic) and aim to drink filtered water.

Amino Acids
GABA - The primary inhibitory neurotransmitter in the brain and central nervous system.
Inhibit nerve impulses to slow the activity of nerve cells and prevent excessive excitatory
processes. Induce relaxation. Improve mood. Support sleep.
Glycine - One of the most important inhibitory neurotransmitters in the CNS, particularly in
the brainstem and spinal cord. Neuroprotective. Support memory and mental function.
Theanine - Increase the release of inhibitory neurotransmitters GABA. Promote relaxation
and improve mood. Bind to glutamate receptors and hinder the associated neuroexcitatory
effects.
Taurine - Act as a modulator of inhibitory neurotransmission. Bind to and activate GABA
and glycine receptors in CNS. Provide anxiolytic and antidepressant effects. Reduce
neuronal excitability and support relaxation.

Amino Acids
Tyrosine - A critical building block (precursor) of catecholamines such as dopamine,

cortisol levels in response to acute or chronic stress. Also an essential nutrient for brain health.
Phosphatidylcholine - A phospholipid. Precursor to acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter essential

DFH IgGI Shield (AI, Under/Over Active, Acute, Chronic)
Mechanism:
• Bovine serum-derived IgG
• Modulates SigA in the gut
• Acts as a biotoxin binder
• Molecule is too large to cross the epithelial walls
When to use:
• Low or high SigA on GI Map
• Steatocrit or calprotectin elevations
• Positive anti-gliadin antibodies
• Positive indican
• Elevated zonulin
Clinical Notes:
• Dairy-free, but not vegan
• May be accepted by health-focused vegans
Alternatives:
• Larch Arabinogalactan
• Mushroom beta-glucans

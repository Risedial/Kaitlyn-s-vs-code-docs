Lipotropic Agents
Lipotropic nutrients promote the removal of fat and inhibit the deposition of lipid
in the liver. They play a crucial role in fat metabolism, liver health, and the
prevention of fatty liver disease by supporting the export of fat from the liver and
enhancing bile flow.

• Choline helps transport and metabolize fats and cholesterol.
• Methionine prevents excess fat buildup in the liver.
• Inositol aids in fat metabolism and helps reduce blood cholesterol.
• Betaine (Trimethylglycine/TMG) helps increase liver glutathione levels while reducing
homocysteine.
• Vitamin B6, B12 and folate are cofactors for the metabolic reactions involving choline,
methionine, and inositol, further assisting in fat metabolism and liver health.
• Burdock contains chlorogenic acid, a key compound to enhance lipid metabolism.
• Chinese Skullcap promotes fatty acid oxidation and helps prevent fatty liver disease.

What is Castor Oil?
Castor oil comes from the seeds of the castor bean plant
(Ricinus communis)
Thick, pale yellow, and rich in ricinoleic acid (≈ 90% of its
fatty acid content)
Ricinoleic acid is what gives castor oil its anti-inflammatory,

Statistical Interpretations
Referencing Laboratories collect data on a population and report wide ranges and list the data
as “normal”
Statistical medians are values where half the population is above and half the population
is below that value
Values are reported only as “High”, “In Range”, or “Low”
“Individual Optimums” may be above, below or equal to the statistical
medians - if no stress is present - and vary from person to person
Genetic potential, type/number of stressors present, duration of
stressors and beneficial stress reduction activity are mitigating factors

Melatonin
We now measure melatonin at noon when it should be low (0-5 pg/ml) so that abnormalities
can be easily identified. Elevations may be due to dysbiosis, chronic stress, high muscle and
organ meat, prolonged exposure to darkness, blood sugar handling issues, insecticides,
herbicides, some chemicals and hypometabolism as part of the stress cascade.
Clients with high levels may appear to have anxiety, stress and
depression, bi-polar disorders and sensitivity to light. Also associated
with lowered Pg/estrogen levels, ovulation issues, low thyroid/adrenal
function, and hypotension. Needless to say, high levels may cause
drowsiness.
Bedtime levels may reach 10-15 pg/ml as pineal gland begins to
produce, reaching a peak of up to 50 pg/ml in the middle of the night.
Low melatonin at bedtime when expected levels are on the rise may
also be due to gut disfunction, pineal gland issues, prolonged exposure to
blue light (computers, etc.)

Secretory Immunoglobulin A (SIgA)
Secretory IgA is a wonderful marker for mucosal barrier health and a general finger pointing at immune system
function or dysfunction; over or under-protection and level of activity. It can be measured throughout the day for
“total” SIgA levels, but AM measurements seem to be the current lab standard.
SIgA is an immunoglobulin (antibody) and part of the adaptive immune system, secreted into the GI tract that
influences the microbiome and helps to maintain mucosal barrier function. It is our first line of immune defense
against antigens and pathogens in the GI and respiratory tracts.
SIgA protects against exposure to food-derived
antigens as well as pathogens. Forms complexes
with gut pathogens and allergens thereby
preventing them from crossing the mucosal
barrier. Levels can be severely impacted by
chronic stress.

AM SIgA Levels (0 – 330)
Low(<75) levels are associated with high or chronic stress whether M/E, physical or chemically induced. Points
finger at impaired mucosal barrier, leaky gut, chronic dysbiosis or infections, SIBO, parasites, chronic exposure to
gliadin and other food antigens, IBD, fight/flight overload. Seen often in immunocompromised clients, protein
malnutrition. Increased risk of intestinal infections and inflammation; very low levels may indicate a “total shut-
down” of immune function in the gut
Equivocal (75 - 145) levels may be considered low depending on the client’s presentation and other lab data.
Poor diet, some drugs, viruses, impaired immune function and chronic stress should always be considered.
High (145 – 330) levels are associated with immune system activation by acute and chronic infections, pathogens,
acute and chronic stress, food allergies and food antigens client is sensitive to. Points the finger at gut dysfunction
and inflammatory conditions. May also point finger at alcoholism smoking, and oral infections.

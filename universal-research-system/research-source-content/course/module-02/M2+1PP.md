Additional Sources of Cortisol
According to research, specific cells within human skin have corticosteroid production and feedback loops that
share important common signaling molecules, receptors, precursors and indeed hormone products with the more
dominant HPA axis.
The enzymes controlling corticosteroid synthesis vary according to the needs of individual cells and tissues, as do
responses to corticosteroid stimulation. This arrangement provides a wide array of control loops from a relatively
small set of common molecules.
The skin of course is the largest organ of the human body, and while the objective of the skin's HPA axis is to
provide for local functional control, the total amount of production does influence the serum levels experienced
by the rest of the body, with estimates varying between 20% and 30% of the total corticosteroid metabolism.
This arrangement points again to the complexity and interplay of metabolic regulatory systems, reminding FDNs to
look for all possible stressors in their assessments to arrive at the most holistic and insightful impressions about
their clients.
Knowing that the skin, including hair follicles, have their own HPA Axis is another reason FDNs never diagnose or
treat “adrenal fatigue”

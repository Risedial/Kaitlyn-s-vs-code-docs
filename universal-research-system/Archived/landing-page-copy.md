# Every time you start a new AI conversation, your research starts over.

---

## What it is

The Universal Research System is a structured research tool that works inside Claude Code. You give it a question or topic, and it works through that topic systematically across as many sessions as you need, building a growing knowledge document that accumulates findings, identifies how key ideas connect, and picks up exactly where you left off every time you return.

---

## The problem it solves

When you use AI for deep research, each new conversation is a blank slate. The AI has no memory of what you found in the last session. If your question is complex enough to require more than one conversation (and the serious ones always are), you face a hard choice: spend half your time reconstructing what you already covered, accept shallow results that fit inside a single session, or abandon the project entirely.

Most researchers end up with scattered notes, missed angles, and no clear record of what they actually discovered. The problem is not intelligence or effort. It is that there is no mechanism for carrying work forward automatically. This system provides that mechanism.

---

## How it works

1. You give the system a question or topic, and it asks you five setup questions to understand what kind of research you want: the scope, the perspectives that matter to you, and what you want to do with the findings.
2. It identifies all the key factors and concepts relevant to your topic, checking each one for relevance before committing to research it.
3. It researches each factor thoroughly, requiring real evidence for every finding before that finding counts, and saves every result to disk after each step so nothing is lost if a session ends.
4. It evaluates how the researched factors connect to each other, requiring independent evidence for each claimed connection rather than inference alone.
5. Once every factor is covered and every connection is evaluated, it synthesizes all findings into a structured knowledge document you can read, search, and ask questions against in future sessions.

---

## What you get

- **Know** exactly where your research stands at any point: the system writes a plain-language summary at the end of every session telling you what was completed and what comes next.
- **Get** a permanent, searchable knowledge document that grows with each session, not a set of scattered notes or a conversation history you cannot return to.
- **Build** a validated map of how key ideas in your topic connect to each other, supported by evidence rather than assertion.
- **Stop** losing context between sessions: every session resumes exactly where the previous one stopped, with no manual reconstruction required.
- **Run** research on topics too complex for a single conversation without sacrificing depth or coverage.

---

## Who it is for

This tool is for researchers, analysts, strategists, and writers who work on questions that are too deep, too multi-layered, or too broad to resolve in a single AI conversation. They have been living with a recurring problem: every time they bring a complex question to an AI, they get either surface-level answers or answers that treat every session as the first one. If your work requires sustained inquiry across many sessions rather than one-shot questions, this system was built for your situation.

---

## How the methodology works

Most AI research tools give you answers. This one builds a record. The difference is that there is a defined method for covering every relevant factor in your question, researching each one to an evidence standard, and confirming every relationship between factors from more than one source before it counts.

1. You give the system your research question, then answer five clarifying questions to establish the scope, the lens you want applied, and the output format you want.
2. The system identifies every factor relevant to your question, tests each one for relevance before committing to research it, and adds only those that make the cut to the research list.
3. It researches each factor one by one, requiring at least three substantive findings per factor, each with the claim, the supporting evidence, and the logical path from evidence to claim written out. A factor is not considered complete until it meets that standard.
4. It tests every pair of researched factors for a structural relationship, classifying each as cause-and-effect, analogy, structural overlap, or correlation.
5. Once all factors are researched and all relationships evaluated, it synthesizes everything into a structured knowledge document and writes a plain-language summary of what was completed and what comes next.
6. When research is complete, you ask any question about the topic and the system answers from the full record. No new research runs.

**What it requires before saying two things are connected**

A normal AI conversation will say two ideas are connected if it has one plausible reason to believe they are. This system requires two independent lines of evidence before it will mark a connection as confirmed, with the supporting evidence and the logical path written out for each. Single-source connections are held out of the final document until a second line is found. The standard exists to prevent the most common failure mode in AI research: a relationship that seems solid because it was argued from one direction and never tested from a second.

**What you end up with**

The result is a structured knowledge document organized by factor and connection, not by conversation turn, that grows with every session and persists accurately across sessions months apart. You can return to it, search it, and ask questions against it without reconstructing what you found last time. It does not expire with the conversation.
